Observations for Level 1: On-Policy Actor-Critic Baselines

Environments: CartPole-v1, LunarLander-v3
Baselines compared: none, avg (running average), value (learned V), gae (Generalized Advantage Estimation)
Seeds: 0, 1, 2 | Total steps: 200,000 | Eval interval: 10,000

CartPole-v1 Results - 

All four baselines eventually reach near-optimal performance (~500) on CartPole,
but they differ in speed and stability.

  none  (no baseline):
    Seeds converge to 500 relatively early (~50k–110k steps depending on seed).
    Seed 1 hits 500 as early as step 20k. Seed 0 takes until ~140k.
    Final returns: 500.0 / 500.0 / 500.0 (all three seeds).

  avg (running-average baseline):
    Slightly more volatile than 'none' mid-training — seed 0 dips to 187 at step
    70k and seed 2 drops to 244 at step 120k before recovering.
    Final returns: 500.0 / 491.8 / 422.6.
    Seed 2 notably fails to fully converge within 200k steps.

  value (learned value function):
    Slower early learning than 'none' — seed 0 stays below 300 until step 60k,
    seed 2 stays below 400 until step 110k.
    Once it learns the value function, convergence is steady and stable.
    Final returns: 500.0 / 500.0 / 481.0.

  gae (Generalized Advantage Estimation):
    The slowest to converge on CartPole among the four. Seed 0 drops to 177 at
    step 140k; seed 2 drops to 300 at step 170k.
    This is somewhat surprising, as GAE is more sophisticated — the instability
    here likely reflects that GAE's bootstrapping benefit is less pronounced on
    the short-horizon CartPole task, and the critic noise adds variance early on.
    Final returns: 461.6 / 466.2 / 418.6.

LunarLander-v3 Results -

LunarLander is significantly harder. None of the four baselines converges to a
positive return within 200k steps. All remain deeply negative throughout.

  none:
    Returns stay in the range [-300, -3965] across all seeds. Seed 2 catastrophi-
    cally diverges to -3965 around step 100k before partially recovering.
    Final returns: -558.1 / -428.1 / -430.3.

  avg:
    Similar range to 'none' but seed 2 collapses to -5793 at step 90k — the
    worst single result across all baselines. The running-average baseline is a
    global scalar and gives poor credit assignment on a task with long, variable
    episodes like LunarLander.
    Final returns: -342.1 / -593.7 / -1074.0.

  value:
    Despite slower convergence seen on CartPole, value does not show catastrophic
    collapses quite as badly. Seeds 0 and 2 stay in the -500 to -900 range by the
    end. Seed 1 remains very negative (~-975).
    Final returns: -538.7 / -975.5 / -902.3.

  gae:
    Mixed results. Seed 0 gradually improves from -320 → -576 by step 160k then
    degrades slightly. Seed 2 shows the clearest improvement trend: starting at
    -1655 and ending at -661.
    Final returns: -1265.4 / -1437.7 / -661.0.
    GAE's multi-step advantage trades off bias for variance in a way that can help
    on longer horizons but requires more steps to see benefit.

Analysis and Conceptual Justification -

1. Baseline hierarchy on CartPole (by stability):
   none ≈ avg > value > gae
   CartPole has short episodes and low-variance returns, so even no baseline
   works well. The raw return signal is already informative.

2. Baseline hierarchy on LunarLander (no winner, but trends):
   gae (seed 2) shows the most consistent improvement trajectory.
   avg suffers from high variance — a single scalar can't capture the
   structure of a 200-step continuous-control episode.
   value is theoretically correct but prone to instability in single-epoch
   on-policy updates (critic can be noisy early and mislead the actor).

3. Variance reduction vs. bias tradeoff:
   - 'none': unbiased but high variance. Works fine on short-horizon problems.
   - 'avg': reduces some variance via EMA baseline, but biased when episodes
     vary widely in length and return magnitude (as in LunarLander).
   - 'value': low bias when V(s) is accurate, significant variance reduction.
     However, learning V in parallel with the policy creates instability.
   - 'gae': interpolates between 1-step TD (low variance, high bias) and
     Monte-Carlo (high variance, low bias) via λ=0.95. On hard tasks it
     provides the best of both worlds given enough data, but requires a
     well-trained critic.

4. Key takeaway:
   On simple tasks all methods work. On harder tasks like LunarLander, 200k
   steps is simply not enough for any on-policy actor-critic variant to solve
   the task. GAE shows the most promise with a clear downward trend in negative
   returns on seed 2. PPO (Level 2) addresses this via mini-batch reuse and
   trust-region clipping, which should show meaningful improvement.
