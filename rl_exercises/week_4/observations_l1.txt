L1 Observations — DQN on CartPole-v1
======================================

Setup
-----
Environment  : CartPole-v1
Training     : 20,000 frames total
Evaluation   : every 1,000 frames
Seeds        : 3 (mean ± std shown as shaded bands in all plots)
Baseline     : hidden_dim=64, num_layers=2, buffer=10000, batch=32, lr=1e-3

Each hyperparameter (width, depth, buffer-size, batch-size) was adapted independently,
while holding all other hyperparameters at their baseline.

The plots can be observed "rl_exercises/week_4/plots/l1/
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Effect of Network Width  [plot_width.png]
   (hidden_dim = 32 / 64 / 256)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
All three widths learn from an initial reward , but differ clearly
in speed and stability across seeds.

- narrow (32): Learns quickly relative to its size, reaching ~250 by
  frame 7000 with a tight std band. However it plateaus early and ends
  around 175, suggesting a capacity ceiling. The network cannot represent
  the Q-function precisely enough for longer episodes.

- baseline (64): Slower initial rise but a steady upward trend throughout.
  The std band widens in later training, indicating increasing seed 
  sensitivity as the agent transitions from easy to harder performance levels.

- wide (256): Consistently the highest-performing configuration. Its mean
  is clearly above the others from frame 7000 onwards. The std band is the
  widest throughout, confirming that the wide network learns faster on
  average but is also the most sensitive to the random seed.

Interpretation: Width provides a real advantage for CartPole. 
The wide network's consistently higher mean is a genuine effect, not 
just a lucky seed. However, the high variance suggests that the large
network is more prone to the moving-target instability: larger Q-value
shifts per gradient update mean that a single bad target estimate can
knock the policy off course. This trades off higher capacity and faster
learning but less stability.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
2. Effect of Network Depth  [plot_depth.png]
   (num_layers = 1 / 2 / 4)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
- shallow (1): High variance early (std band is wide), reflecting that
  a single linear layer is sensitive to initialisation. The mean 
  stabilises around 120–140 before showing steady improvement, ending 
  at ~225. Despite its simplicity it remains competitive.

- baseline (2): The most consistent improver. Its std band is relatively
  small throughout and its mean climbs steadily, peaking at ~365 near frame
  18000. This is the best final performance of the three depth configs.

- deep (4): Shows the highest early variance, indicating that some 
  seeds learn fast initially while others barely improve. After frame
  7000 the mean settles into a plateau, well below the baseline, and only 
  recovers slightly at the very end. Extra depth consistently hurts
  mid-training performance here.

Interpretation: CartPole has a 4-dimensional state space and a smooth,
relatively simple Q-function. Extra layers add no benefit but do add 
more parameters to optimise (slowing convergence). The fact that
depth=2 outperforms depth=4 across all 3 seeds (their std bands barely 
overlap after frame 8000) makes this a statistically credible conclusion
even with 3 seeds.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Effect of Replay Buffer Size  [plot_buffer.png]
   (capacity = 500 / 10000 / 50000)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
- tiny (500): Reaches a mean of ~235 by frame 5000 (the fastest early
  learner), but with an enormous std band. That means some seeds collapse 
  while others perform well. After frame 5000 the mean declines steadily. 
  This is the only configuration with mean reward falling over time.

- baseline (10000): Steady and consistent improvement throughout. Peaks
  at ~365 around frame 17500 and ends at ~290. Its std band is moderate
  and widens only in late training.

- large (50000): The smoothest trajectory of all configurations across
  the entire experiment. The std band remains relatively tight throughout 
  and the mean climbs reliably from ~150 at frame 5000 to ~275 by frame 20000.
  It never peaks as high as the baseline but never collapses either.

Interpretation: Buffer size is a important hyperparameter.
DQN requires i.i.d. samples to produce stable estimates. A buffer of
only 500 with batch size 32 means the network repeatedly resamples the
same recent transitions, violating this assumption. The huge early variance
(some seeds do well, others fail immediately) shows that performance is 
essentially a function of whether the first few hundred transitions happen to 
be diverse. The inevitable mean decline after frame 5000 reflects the policy 
overfitting to recent experience and forgetting earlier behaviour.
The large buffer prevents this but slows learning because it dilutes
recent, higher-quality transitions with a large volume of old, random-
policy data. A buffer of 10000 provides the best balance between
diversity and recency within the 20000-frame budget.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Effect of Batch Size  [plot_batch.png]
   (batch = 8 / 32 / 256, buffer fixed at 10000)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
- small (8): Reaches ~230 early, but with a large std band.
  Settles around 175–210 in the middle phase and ends at ~260.

- baseline (32): Steady improvement, ends at ~295 with a moderate std
  band.

- large (256): Biggest std band across frames, with high variance throughout.
  The band extends to 500 near frame 20000, driven by at least one seed 
  achieving near-maximum reward. Mean ends highest at ~410, but this late
  divergence is not reliable across seeds.

Interpretation: The three std bands overlap for virtually the entire
training run, confirming that batch size has the smallest effect of the
four axes studied. No configuration is distinguishable from the others
with statistical confidence at 3 seeds. The large batch's late-training
mean advantage is interesting but its equally large std band means it
is not a reliable improvement (it may reflect one lucky seed). The main
difference large batches are can diverge later.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Summary and Key Takeaways
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. All configurations successfully learn, improving from ~10 to 150–440
   across 20000 frames.

2. No configuration has fully converged by 20000 frames; all mean curves
   are still trending upward at the end, suggesting longer training would
   reveal clearer differences.

3. The tiny buffer (500) is a clearly bad, as it declines over time.

4. Width helps but increases seed sensitivity. The wide network is 
   consistently the best-performing configuration on average, but
   also the most prone to large inter-seed variance.

5. Depth provides no benefit for this environment and actively hurts
   mid-training performance at depth=4.
