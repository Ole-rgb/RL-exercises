Initial Observation:

since by default, the Q value for state-action pairs are initialized to zero,
and the epsilon is kept at 0.1, it was not exploring much, due to which the agent required
very large training steps to even discover the final reward.

the gamma was set to 1, which meant that the agent was focusing on future rewards/Q vals, which were not there initially.
even with gamma 1, since the agent was just going left because of 0 initialization of the Q, and np.argmax picking the first 0
the agent only went left and not right, where the actual high reward was.

Even increasing the exploration factor to a higher 0.5 didn't help as much.
The failure here was probably due to a lack of epsilon scheduling, as the policy was always epsilon-greedy exploring,
but greedy exploiting might have been a better way after the agent has already found some good reward signal.

Since sarsa was not doing the max on the next state, it was almost hopeless for it to achieve anything good
with current configuration.
